# ArcherDB Trademark Guidelines

This document outlines the trademark policy and branding guidelines for the ArcherDB project.

## Trademark Ownership

"ArcherDB" and the ArcherDB logo are trademarks of the ArcherDB project contributors.

## Permitted Uses

### You MAY use the ArcherDB name to:

1. **Refer to the project**: Describe ArcherDB in articles, blog posts, documentation, and technical discussions

2. **State compatibility**: Indicate that your software or service is compatible with ArcherDB (e.g., "Compatible with ArcherDB" or "Works with ArcherDB")

3. **Reference community involvement**: Identify yourself as an ArcherDB user, contributor, or community member

4. **Link to the project**: Include links to the official ArcherDB repositories and documentation

5. **Educational purposes**: Use in tutorials, courses, and educational materials about ArcherDB

### You MAY NOT use the ArcherDB name to:

1. **Imply endorsement**: Suggest that your product or service is officially endorsed, sponsored, or affiliated with the ArcherDB project without explicit permission

2. **Create confusion**: Use in a way that might confuse users about the source or origin of a product or service

3. **Modified distributions**: Name a modified version of ArcherDB in a way that suggests it is the official ArcherDB project

4. **Commercial products**: Use as the primary name of a commercial product or service without permission

5. **Domain names**: Register domain names containing "archerdb" without permission

## Logo Usage

### Official Logo

The official ArcherDB logo may be used under the following conditions:

- **Unmodified**: The logo must be used in its original form without modification
- **Clear space**: Maintain adequate clear space around the logo
- **Minimum size**: Do not display the logo smaller than readable size
- **Color integrity**: Use the official color scheme or approved single-color variants
- **No distortion**: Do not stretch, rotate, or otherwise distort the logo

### Logo Colors

Primary colors:
- Primary: #2D5A27 (Forest Green)
- Secondary: #4A90A4 (Geographic Blue)
- Accent: #E8B84A (Precision Gold)

## Naming Guidelines

### Forks and Derivatives

If you fork or create a derivative of ArcherDB:

- **Choose a distinct name** that does not include "ArcherDB"
- **Clearly indicate** in your documentation that it is a derivative work
- **Maintain attribution** as required by the Apache License 2.0

### Integrations and Extensions

For products that integrate with ArcherDB:

- **Use descriptive phrases**: "for ArcherDB" or "with ArcherDB support"
- **Avoid implying official status**: Do not use phrases like "Official ArcherDB Extension"
- **Example acceptable names**:
  - "MyApp for ArcherDB"
  - "MyApp with ArcherDB Support"
  - "MyApp - ArcherDB Compatible"

### Unacceptable Names

- "ArcherDB Enterprise" (implies official product)
- "ArcherDB Plus" (implies official variant)
- "Official ArcherDB Tool" (implies official endorsement)
- "archerdb.io" domain (without permission)

## Attribution Requirements

When using ArcherDB in your project:

1. **Include NOTICE file contents** as required by Apache License 2.0
2. **Acknowledge TigerBeetle where required** for derivative-work attribution (see NOTICE file)
3. **Link to the official project** when practical

## Upstream Acknowledgment

ArcherDB incorporates derivative work and implementation ideas from
TigerBeetle. When discussing ArcherDB's architecture or heritage:

- Acknowledge TigerBeetle as upstream where attribution is relevant
- Do not misrepresent upstream VSR/storage work as original ArcherDB work
- Reference TigerBeetle documentation for upstream consensus and storage details

See the [NOTICE](NOTICE) file for complete attribution requirements.

## Requesting Permission

For uses not covered by these guidelines, or to request permission for trademark use:

1. Open an issue in the [ArcherDB repository](https://github.com/ArcherDB-io/archerdb/issues)
2. Describe your intended use
3. Wait for maintainer response before proceeding

## Updates to These Guidelines

These guidelines may be updated. Check this document periodically for changes.

---

*These guidelines are intended to protect the ArcherDB brand while enabling the community to discuss, use, and extend the project. When in doubt, ask.*
